Week 4 – Task 1: 
Discussing Study or Career Plans with an Advisor
You said:
Hi, I’d like to talk about my study plan and get some advice.
English Conversational Partner said:
Of course. What’s been on your mind about your studies?
You said:
I’m studying business, but I’m not sure what direction I want to take after I graduate.
English Conversational Partner said:
I see. Are you unsure about your career options, or about whether business is the right degree for you?
You said:
Um… both, actually.
I mean, I like some subjects, but I don’t really see the connection to jobs.
English Conversational Partner said:
That makes sense. Which subjects do you enjoy the most?
You said:
I enjoy marketing classes. They feel more creative.
English Conversational Partner said:
Okay, so the creative side interests you more.
Have you thought about internships or work experience in that area?
You said:
I have, but I wasn’t sure if I was ready yet.
English Conversational Partner said:
When you say “not ready”, do you mean skills, confidence, or time?
You said:
Confidence, mostly.
I think I worried you meant skills at first.
English Conversational Partner said:
Thanks for clarifying. Confidence is a very common concern.
Sometimes short-term internships can help build that gradually.
You said:
That sounds better than something long.
English Conversational Partner said:
Exactly. You don’t have to commit to anything big straight away.
You said:
Okay. That makes me feel a bit less stressed.

Week 4 – Task 2: 
Role Play: Discussing Group Work Roles with a Teammate
You said:
Okay, let’s do the role play. You’re my teammate.
I want to talk about our group project.
English Conversational Partner said:
Sure. What do you want to discuss about the project?
You said:
I feel like we haven’t really decided who does which part yet.
English Conversational Partner said:
Yeah, I feel the same. Everything feels a bit unclear.
What do you think we should do first?
You said:
I think we should assign roles, so everyone knows their responsibility.
English Conversational Partner said:
That sounds reasonable. Do you already know which role you’d prefer?
You said:
I’m comfortable doing research and writing, but I’m not confident with presenting.
English Conversational Partner said:
Okay, so you’d rather not present.
Do you mean you don’t want to present at all?
You said:
Not exactly.
I mean… I can present if needed, but I prefer not to.
English Conversational Partner said:
Got it. Thanks for explaining.
You could say that you’re happy to help, but that research and writing are your strengths.
You said:
Yes, that sounds better than just saying no.
English Conversational Partner said:
We could also suggest a small deadline so the project doesn’t get delayed.
You said:
When you say small deadline, do you mean this week or next week?
English Conversational Partner said:
I was thinking by the end of next week.
You said:
Okay, that sounds manageable.
English Conversational Partner said:
So you’ll suggest roles and a deadline, and we’ll see how the group responds.
You said:
Yes. I feel more confident about talking to them now.
