Week 3 – Task 2: Reporting a Lost Item or Filing a Minor Police Report
You said:
Hi, um, I want to report a lost item.
I’m not sure if I’m in the right place, but I was told to come here first.
English Conversational Partner said:
That’s okay, you’re in the right place. What item have you lost?
You said:
My backpack.
I realised it was missing last night when I came back home.
English Conversational Partner said:
Alright. Can you tell me where you last remember having it?
You said:
I think at the university library.
I stayed there quite late, so maybe around six or six-thirty.
English Conversational Partner said:
Okay. Which library was that?
You said:
The main library.
Sorry, I don’t know the English name exactly, but the biggest one on campus.
English Conversational Partner said:
That’s fine, I understand. Can you describe the backpack for me?
You said:
It’s black, medium size.
Inside there was my laptop, some notebooks, and charger.
English Conversational Partner said:
Do you know the brand of the backpack?
You said:
The brand…
I know the logo, but I don’t really know how to say it in English.
English Conversational Partner said:
That’s okay. Any other identifying features?
You said:
Yes, there is a small Korean flag keychain on the zip.
English Conversational Partner said:
That’s helpful. Was there anything particularly valuable inside?
You said:
Yes, the laptop is quite expensive.
Also my student ID card was inside.
English Conversational Partner said:
In that case, I would recommend filing a formal report.
You said:
Formal report meaning police report?
English Conversational Partner said:
Yes, that’s correct.
You said:
Okay.
I wasn’t sure if it’s necessary, but if you recommend, I’ll do that.
English Conversational Partner said:
It helps in case the item is found or if you need documentation later.
You said:
Documentation meaning proof?
English Conversational Partner said:
Yes, proof that you reported it lost.
You said:
Ah okay.
That makes sense.
English Conversational Partner said:
I’ll need some details from you. When exactly did you last see the backpack?
You said:
I used it at the library before dinner.
Then I think I packed everything, but maybe I forgot it on the chair.
English Conversational Partner said:
So you’re not completely sure where it was left?
You said:
Yes, I’m not one hundred percent sure.
That’s why I feel a bit stupid about this.
English Conversational Partner said:
Don’t worry, this happens quite often.
You said:
Okay, that makes me feel better.
English Conversational Partner said:
Can I take your contact details so we can reach you if it’s found?
You said:
Yes.
Email is better than phone, because sometimes I miss calls.
English Conversational Partner said:
No problem. I’ll record your email address.
You said:
Thank you.
Can I ask something else?
English Conversational Partner said:
Of course.
You said:
If someone finds it, do they bring it here, or to the library?
English Conversational Partner said:
Usually to the library first, and then they notify us.
You said:
Okay.
So should I also check with the library directly?
English Conversational Partner said:
Yes, that’s a good idea.
You said:
Alright. I’ll do both then.
English Conversational Partner said:
Do you have ID with you today?
You said:
Passport is okay?
English Conversational Partner said:
Yes, that’s fine.
You said:
Okay.
Thank you for explaining everything clearly. I was a bit nervous.

Week 3 – Task 3: Requesting Maintenance or Repairs in a Rental Property
You said:
Hi, I’m calling because there’s a problem in my apartment, and I’m not sure what to do.
English Conversational Partner said:
That’s okay, I can help. What seems to be the issue?
You said:
The heater in my bedroom is not working properly.
English Conversational Partner said:
Can you tell me what exactly is happening with it?
You said:
It turns on, like the light is on, but there’s no warm air coming out.
English Conversational Partner said:
Alright. When did you first notice this problem?
You said:
Last night.
It was quite cold, so I tried several times.
English Conversational Partner said:
Have you adjusted the thermostat?
You said:
Thermostat… sorry, can you explain what that is?
English Conversational Partner said:
The temperature control on the wall.
You said:
Ah okay.
Yes, I changed the number higher, but nothing changed.
English Conversational Partner said:
Did you hear any unusual noise from the heater?
You said:
No noise.
Just silence, which feels wrong.
English Conversational Partner said:
That does sound like it needs checking. We can organise a technician.
You said:
Is it possible for someone to come today?
English Conversational Partner said:
It’s unlikely today, but tomorrow morning is possible.
You said:
Tomorrow is okay.
I just wanted to check because the room is very cold at night.
English Conversational Partner said:
I understand. Is the heater in other rooms working?
You said:
Actually, living room heater works fine.
Only bedroom one is not working.
English Conversational Partner said:
That’s useful information.
You said:
Okay.
Do I need to stay home when the technician comes?
English Conversational Partner said:
Yes, or you can leave a key with reception.
You said:
I think I’ll stay home.
But can they call before coming?
English Conversational Partner said:
Yes, I can request that.
You said:
Please do, because sometimes I’m in the shower and don’t hear knock.
English Conversational Partner said:
No problem. I’ll note that.
You said:
One more question, sorry.
English Conversational Partner said:
That’s fine.
You said:
If the heater is broken, do I have to pay for repair?
English Conversational Partner said:
No, heating is the landlord’s responsibility.
You said:
Okay, good.
I was worried about extra cost.
English Conversational Partner said:
You don’t need to worry about that.
You said:
Alright.
So tomorrow morning, technician comes, they call before, and I stay home.
English Conversational Partner said:
That’s correct.
You said:
Thank you very much.
Winter here is harder than I expected.
English Conversational Partner said:
I understand. We’ll get it sorted as soon as possible.
