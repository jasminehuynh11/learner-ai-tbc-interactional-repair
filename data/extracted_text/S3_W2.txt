Task 1: Visiting a Medical Clinic and Booking or Changing an Appointment
My Words: Hi, excuse me. I want to see a doctor.
English Conversational Partner said: Of course! Absolutely, you’ve come to the right place. Let me just help you with that. So you’re looking to book an appointment with one of our doctors today or another day?
My Words: Uh, yes. New one.
English Conversational Partner said: Perfect, no problem at all — we see new patients all the time. Before I look at the schedule, I’ll just ask a couple of quick questions so I can book you in properly. What seems to be going on?
My Words: Stomach. Something strange.
English Conversational Partner said: Okay, stomach issues — thank you for telling me. When you say “something strange”, are you feeling pain, discomfort, bloating, or maybe a burning feeling?
My Words: Bloating. And sometimes burning.
English Conversational Partner said: Got it — bloating and sometimes a burning sensation. That’s really helpful information. And has that been happening for a few days, or longer than that?
My Words: Two days. Maybe three.
English Conversational Partner said: Okay, two to three days — that gives us a good idea. Now let me just check what appointments we have available. Alright… just a moment… okay.
My Words: Okay.
English Conversational Partner said: So today we’re actually fully booked, unfortunately. But the good news is we do have availability tomorrow, and we can usually find something that works around work schedules as well.
My Words: Tomorrow is okay.
English Conversational Partner said: Great. So tomorrow we have a couple of options. The first one is in the morning at 9:40am, and the second one is later at 11:20am. Let me know which one sounds better for you.
My Words: 9:40… No. I work at ten.
English Conversational Partner said: Ah yes, that would be cutting it very close — you’re right to flag that. In that case, the 11:20am appointment would be much more comfortable and less stressful.
My Words: Yes. 11:20.
English Conversational Partner said: Perfect. I’ll lock that in for you. Now I’ll just grab a few details to complete the booking. Can I take your full name, please?
My Words: James Lee.
English Conversational Partner said: Lovely, thank you. And just to double-check the spelling — that’s J-A-M-E-S, L-E-E, is that right?
My Words: Yes.
English Conversational Partner said: Great, all sorted. Now, do you have Medicare, or are you covered by Overseas Student Health Cover?
My Words: Student insurance. OSHC.
English Conversational Partner said: Perfect — OSHC is absolutely fine here. Just make sure you bring your card with you tomorrow so we can process everything smoothly.
My Words: Okay.
English Conversational Partner said: And just so you know what to expect, you’ll pay for the appointment after you see the doctor, and then you can submit a claim to your insurance provider if you need to.
My Words: Claim later?
English Conversational Partner said: Yes, exactly — pay first, then claim afterward. A lot of students do it that way.
My Words: Okay.
English Conversational Partner said: We also ask patients to arrive about ten minutes early. That just gives you time to fill out a short paper form at reception — nothing complicated, just basic details.
My Words: Paper is fine.
English Conversational Partner said: Great, that makes things easy. Now, just one more thing — during the appointment, you can absolutely ask the doctor about medication, tests, or anything else you’re worried about.
My Words: Test also? Like blood?
English Conversational Partner said: Yes, if the doctor thinks it’s necessary, they can arrange blood tests or other checks. And if you need a medical certificate for work, you can request that as well.
My Words: Yes. Medical certificate.
English Conversational Partner said: No problem at all — very common request. Alright, let me just recap everything so we’re on the same page. You’re booked in for tomorrow at 11:20am. Please bring your OSHC card, arrive about ten minutes early, and let the doctor know about the bloating and burning after meals.
My Words: Tomorrow. 11:20. Okay.
English Conversational Partner said: Perfect. You’re all set. If anything changes or your symptoms get worse tonight, don’t hesitate to seek urgent care. Otherwise, we’ll see you tomorrow.
My Words: Okay. Thank you.
















Task 3: Enquiring About a Rental Property or Housing Inspection
My Words: Hello. I’m calling about an apartment.
English Conversational Partner said: Hi there! Absolutely, thanks for calling. I can definitely help you with that. Let me just check — are you calling about a one-bedroom property near Central Station?
My Words: Yes. That one.
English Conversational Partner said: Perfect, I know exactly which one you’re referring to. That’s a popular listing, so it’s good you called early. Are you mainly looking for the inspection details today?
My Words: Yes. When can I see it?
English Conversational Partner said: Great question. We have an inspection scheduled for this Saturday, and it runs from 10am until 10:30am. So you don’t need to arrive exactly at 10 — anytime within that window is fine.
My Words: 10 to 10:30… okay.
English Conversational Partner said: Exactly. Some people like to come early, some come closer to the end — both are completely fine. Just one important thing before I forget: you’ll need to register online before attending.
My Words: Register… email only?
English Conversational Partner said: Yes, just your name and email address. No full account, no documents at this stage — very quick and simple.
My Words: Okay. That’s fine.
English Conversational Partner said: Perfect. Now, just so I give you the most helpful information — are you currently renting in Australia, or would this be your first rental here?
My Words: I rent now. But short time.
English Conversational Partner said: That’s completely fine — a lot of people are in the same situation. Short rental history is very common, especially for students and people who’ve recently moved.
My Words: Okay.
English Conversational Partner said: Let me also run through a few key details people usually ask about before inspections. First, the apartment is unfurnished, so you would need your own bed and furniture.
My Words: No bed?
English Conversational Partner said: That’s right — no bed or sofa. However, the kitchen appliances are included, so you’ll have a fridge, oven, and cooktop.
My Words: Fridge included. Good.
English Conversational Partner said: Yes, that definitely makes things easier. Now, in terms of noise, because it’s close to Central Station, there can be some train noise during the day.
My Words: At night also?
English Conversational Partner said: Mostly during the day and early evening. Late at night it’s generally much quieter, but it’s good to be aware of.
My Words: Okay.
English Conversational Partner said: There’s no dedicated parking space with the apartment, just so you know. But there is street parking available nearby.
My Words: I don’t have car.
English Conversational Partner said: Perfect — that makes parking a non-issue then. Now, the rent for the property is $510 per week, and the bond is four weeks’ rent.
My Words: Four weeks… so bond is… wait.
English Conversational Partner said: That comes to $2,040 in total for the bond.
My Words: Yes. Correct.
English Conversational Partner said: Great. If you like the property after the inspection, you can apply straight away online. Applications are usually reviewed within two to three business days.
My Words: If many people apply… still chance?
English Conversational Partner said: Yes, definitely. What helps most is having your documents ready — things like photo ID, proof of income, and any rental history you have.
My Words: Rental history… I have only short one.
English Conversational Partner said: That’s okay — you can still apply. The agent will look at the full application, not just one factor.
My Words: Okay.
English Conversational Partner said: So just to quickly summarise everything for you: inspection is this Saturday from 10 to 10:30am, please register online beforehand with your name and email, and if you like the place, you can apply online afterward.
My Words: Saturday. Register first. Then decide.
English Conversational Partner said: Exactly right. If you think of any other questions before Saturday, feel free to give us a call.
My Words: Okay. Thank you.
English Conversational Partner said: You’re very welcome! Good luck with the inspection — we’ll see you on Saturday.
