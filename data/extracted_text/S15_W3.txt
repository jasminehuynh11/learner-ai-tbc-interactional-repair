Week 3 – Task 1: 
Internet Not Working at Home
You said:
Hi, I’m calling because my internet connection stopped working this afternoon.
English Conversational Partner said:
I’m sorry to hear that. Let’s see what’s happening. When did you first notice the problem?
You said:
It was about an hour ago. I was working online, and then everything suddenly disconnected.
English Conversational Partner said:
Okay, thanks for explaining. Have you tried restarting your modem or router yet?
You said:
Yes, I restarted both of them, but it didn’t solve the problem.
English Conversational Partner said:
Alright. When you look at the modem now, do you see any unusual lights?
You said:
Yes, there’s a red blinking light. Normally it’s green.
English Conversational Partner said:
Just to check, is it blinking quickly or slowly?
You said:
Um, slowly. At first I thought it was fast, but it’s actually slow blinking.
English Conversational Partner said:
Thanks for clarifying. That helps. A slow blinking red light usually means a connection issue outside your home.
Let me check if there are any service problems in your area.
You said:
Okay, because I wasn’t sure if it was something I did wrong.
English Conversational Partner said:
I understand. I’ve checked, and there is a temporary outage affecting your area.
You said:
Oh, I see. So it’s not only my apartment?
English Conversational Partner said:
That’s right. It’s not just your connection.
You said:
Okay. So I don’t need to restart it again now, right?
English Conversational Partner said:
Correct. Restarting again won’t help at the moment.
You said:
Alright. Do you know roughly how long it might take to fix?
English Conversational Partner said:
The current estimate is about two hours.
You said:
Two hours… okay. I’ll check again later tonight.
English Conversational Partner said:
That sounds good. If it’s still not working after that, please contact us again.
You said:
Thank you for explaining everything.

Week 3 – Task 2: 
Reporting a Lost Mobile Phone
You said:
Hi, I’d like to report a lost phone.
English Conversational Partner said:
Of course. When did you last see your phone?
You said:
I think it was this morning, just before I got on the train.
English Conversational Partner said:
Alright. Was that at the station or on the train itself?
You said:
At the station. Near the platform, I think.
English Conversational Partner said:
Okay. Can you describe the phone for me?
You said:
It’s black with a dark blue case. The screen has a small crack on one corner.
English Conversational Partner said:
Thank you. Is it an Android phone or an iPhone?
You said:
It’s an iPhone.
Sorry, I should have said that earlier.
English Conversational Partner said:
No problem at all. Have you tried calling the phone or using a tracking app?

I tried calling it, but it seems to be switched off now.
English Conversational Partner said:
Alright. In that case, we can register it as lost.
If someone finds it and hands it in, we’ll contact you.

Okay. Do you need my phone number for that?

Actually, your email address would be best.
You said:
Oh, okay. I thought phone number first.
I’ll give you my email.

Great, thank you.
I’d also recommend contacting your mobile provider to block the SIM card.

Block the SIM… you mean so no one can use it?

Exactly, to protect your personal information.

Okay, I’ll do that today.

Is there anything else you’d like help with?

No, that’s everything. Thank you.
