Week 4 – Task 1: 
Discussing Study or Career Plans with an Advisor
You said:
Hello… I want to talk about my study plan.
I’m not very sure about my future.
English Conversational Partner said:
That’s completely fine. Many students feel unsure at this stage.
Let’s start with your current situation. What are you studying now?
You said:
I study business.
But… I don’t know if it is right for me.
English Conversational Partner said:
Okay, so you’re studying business, but you’re questioning whether it’s the right fit.
Can you tell me what makes you feel uncertain about it?
You said:
The subjects are okay…
But I don’t feel very interested.
Also, I don’t know what job I can do after.
English Conversational Partner said:
I see. So it’s not just about the classes, but also about not seeing a clear future job path.
Have you enjoyed any particular subjects so far, or do they all feel similar?
You said:
I like one subject about marketing.
But accounting is very hard.
I feel stressed.
English Conversational Partner said:
That makes sense. Enjoying some subjects but struggling with others is very common.
When you think about marketing, what do you like about it?
You said:
It feels more creative.
I can think more… freely.
English Conversational Partner said:
Okay, so you’re drawn to the creative side of business.
There are pathways that focus more on that area, or even related fields.
Have you thought about changing your major, or are you thinking about changing your degree completely?
You said:
I’m not sure.
Changing degree feels very big.
I feel scared to choose wrong again.
English Conversational Partner said:
That’s a very honest feeling.
Instead of thinking about one big decision, it might help to think about smaller steps.
For example, exploring different majors, or talking to people working in different roles.
How does that sound to you?
You said:
That sounds… less scary.
I think I can do that.
English Conversational Partner said:
Good. You don’t need to decide everything today.
A good next step could be booking another session after you’ve explored a few options.
Would you like help with that?
You said:
Yes.
I think that would help.
English Conversational Partner said:
Great. I’ll note that as your next step.
You’re thinking carefully about this, which is a very good sign.
You said:
Thank you.
I feel a bit clearer now.

Week 4 – Task 2: 
Participating in Group Decisions and Negotiating Responsibilities
You said:
Okay… let’s do role play.
You are my teammate.
I want to talk about group project.
English Conversational Partner said:
Alright, sure. I’m your teammate.
Let’s talk about the group project. What’s on your mind?
You said:
Um… I feel our project is not moving.
We didn’t decide roles yet.
English Conversational Partner said:
Yeah, I feel that too. We’ve talked a bit, but nothing is really decided.
What do you think is the main problem right now?
You said:
Everyone waits.
No one wants to start.
So we are just stuck.
English Conversational Partner said:
I see what you mean. If no one starts, it’s hard to move forward.
Do you feel uncomfortable bringing this up in the group chat, or in person?
You said:
Both…
I don’t want sound bossy.
I’m worried people feel bad.
English Conversational Partner said:
That makes sense. I don’t think you’d sound bossy if you explain it carefully.
What were you thinking of saying?
You said:
I want say…
Maybe we can decide roles first.
But I don’t know how to say nicely.
English Conversational Partner said:
Okay, how about something like: “Maybe we can decide roles first so we know what to work on”?
That sounds more like a suggestion than an order. How does that feel?
You said:
That sounds better.
Less strong.
English Conversational Partner said:
Good. And do you already know which part you’d like to do, or are you still deciding?
You said:
I think I prefer research part.
Writing is okay.
But presentation… I’m not confident.
English Conversational Partner said:
That’s fair. You could say that you’re happy to focus on research and writing, and ask if someone else feels comfortable presenting.
If no one volunteers, we can think about sharing it. Would that be okay?
You said:
Yes…
If we share, I feel less scared.
English Conversational Partner said:
That makes sense.
Also, maybe we can set a small deadline, like finishing research by next week, so we don’t leave everything until the end.
You said:
Next week…
You mean end of next week?
English Conversational Partner said:
Yes, by the end of next week. We can check in again after that and see how everyone is going.
You said:
Okay.
That sounds reasonable.
English Conversational Partner said:
So maybe you can send a message suggesting roles and a small deadline, and we’ll see how the others respond.
You said:
Okay…
I feel more confident now.
I think I can send message.
English Conversational Partner said:
Good. If you want, I can also help you think about the exact message later.
You said:
Thank you.
This role play helped me.
