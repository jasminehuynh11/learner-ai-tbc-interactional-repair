Task 2: Reporting a Lost Item or Filing a Minor Police Report 
My Words: Hi, excuse me. I think I lost something.
English Conversational Partner said: That’s okay, take your time. You’ve come to the right place. We deal with lost items all the time. First, I just need to check — do you believe the item was lost, or do you think it might have been stolen?
My Words: Lost. I think lost. But I’m not totally sure.
English Conversational Partner said: That’s completely fine. You don’t need to be one hundred percent certain to make a report. We can still record it as lost, and if anything changes later, it can be updated. What item are you missing?
My Words: My backpack. Small one.
English Conversational Partner said: Alright, a small backpack. Thank you. Let me enter that. Now, can you tell me when you last clearly remember having it with you?
My Words: Yesterday. After class.
English Conversational Partner said: Yesterday after class — that’s helpful. And after class, do you remember where you went? Even roughly is okay.
My Words: Library. Then coffee. Then train. Many places.
English Conversational Partner said: That’s okay, that happens a lot. I’ll note multiple possible locations. Now, let’s describe the backpack a bit more so it’s easier to identify if someone hands it in. What colour is it?
My Words: Dark blue.
English Conversational Partner said: Dark blue, got it. Does it have any logos, patterns, or something attached to it?
My Words: Yes. Keychain. Cartoon face.
English Conversational Partner said: That’s actually very helpful. I’ll include that detail. People often remember keychains more than the bag itself. Now, can you tell me what was inside the backpack?
My Words: Laptop. Charger. Notebook. Lunch box.
English Conversational Partner said: Okay, laptop, charger, notebook, and a lunch box. Because there’s a laptop inside, it’s especially important that we record this properly. Do you remember the brand or size of the laptop?
My Words: Silver one. Medium size. I don’t know brand.
English Conversational Partner said: That’s okay — silver and medium size is still useful. Now, have you already contacted any of the places you visited, like the library or café?
My Words: Library, yes. They don’t have it. Café, not yet.
English Conversational Partner said: Alright. I recommend checking with the café later today as well. Sometimes items are found a few hours later. Filing this report now is still the right thing to do. Now I’ll need your name and contact details so we can reach you if the backpack is found.
My Words: Daniel Lee. Email is better.
English Conversational Partner said: Email is absolutely fine. Please spell it out slowly for me so I can enter it correctly.
My Words: D-A-N-I-E-L dot L-E-E at gmail dot com.
English Conversational Partner said: Great, thank you. I’ve entered that. Now, just so you know how the process works, if the item is handed in to any police station or transport office, they’ll check the system and contact you using this email.
My Words: Okay.
English Conversational Partner said: I also want to let you know that unfortunately not all lost items are recovered, but backpacks with laptops are often returned, especially if they’re left in public places like libraries or trains.
My Words: If I need proof for university… this report is okay?
English Conversational Partner said: Yes, exactly. This report can be used as official proof that you reported the loss, which universities usually accept for things like deadline extensions or replacement cards.
My Words: Okay. That helps.
English Conversational Partner said: Good. Now, before I finalise everything, I’ll quickly read the details back to you, just to make sure I’ve recorded it correctly. You lost a small dark blue backpack with a cartoon keychain yesterday after class, possibly at the library, a café, or on the train. Inside were a silver laptop, charger, notebook, and lunch box. Does that sound right?
My Words: Yes. That’s right.
English Conversational Partner said: Perfect. I’m finalising the report now. Here is your report number. Please keep it somewhere safe, as you may need it later.
My Words: Okay.
English Conversational Partner said: I also strongly recommend cancelling or securing any accounts connected to the laptop, just in case, and checking with the café today.
My Words: Yes. I will.
English Conversational Partner said: Alright. If you remember any new details later — even something small — you can contact us again and update the report.
My Words: Okay. Thank you.
English Conversational Partner said: You’re welcome. I hope the backpack turns up soon. Take care.
















Task 3: Requesting Maintenance or Repairs in a Rental Property
My Words: Hi, I’m calling about a problem in my apartment.
English Conversational Partner said: Hi there! No problem at all, thanks for calling. I can help you with maintenance issues. Before we get into the details, can I just confirm the address of the property so I’m looking at the right file?
My Words: Yes. 24 King Street. Unit 603.
English Conversational Partner said: Perfect, thank you. I’ve got that open now. Alright, what seems to be the issue today?
My Words: The bathroom. Something is wrong.
English Conversational Partner said: Okay, the bathroom — thanks for letting me know. When you say something is wrong, is it related to water, electricity, or something else?
My Words: Water. But not always.
English Conversational Partner said: Alright, water-related but intermittent — that’s helpful. Can you tell me what actually happens when the problem occurs?
My Words: Shower is okay. Then suddenly cold. Then hot again.
English Conversational Partner said: Okay, so the water temperature changes suddenly while you’re showering. That can definitely be frustrating. Does this happen every time you shower, or only sometimes?
My Words: Not every time. Mostly at night.
English Conversational Partner said: Mostly in the evenings — that’s an important detail. That can sometimes be related to water pressure or the system heating up. I’ll make a note of that. When did you first notice this happening?
My Words: Maybe two weeks ago. But before it was small.
English Conversational Partner said: Okay, so it started about two weeks ago and has gradually become more noticeable. Have you changed anything recently, like adjusting the hot water settings or using a new appliance?
My Words: No. Nothing new.
English Conversational Partner said: That’s good to know. Just to check, when the water goes cold, does it stay cold for a long time, or does it return to hot fairly quickly?
My Words: Few seconds. But sometimes scary.
English Conversational Partner said: I understand — even a few seconds of cold water can be unpleasant, especially unexpectedly. I’ll make sure the technician is aware that the temperature changes suddenly. Now, is this happening in the shower only, or also in the sink?
My Words: Sink is okay. Only shower.
English Conversational Partner said: Perfect, that helps narrow it down. That suggests it’s likely related to the shower unit rather than the whole apartment. I’ll log it that way so the right person attends.
My Words: Okay.
English Conversational Partner said: Just so you know how the process works, I’ll submit this as a non-urgent maintenance request today. A plumber or technician will usually contact you within one to two business days to arrange a time to inspect it.
My Words: They call me?
English Conversational Partner said: Yes, they usually call or text first to organise access. Would weekday daytime access be okay, or do you prefer evenings?
My Words: Evening is better. After work.
English Conversational Partner said: No problem at all — I’ll note evening access preferred. And just to double-check, is there any leaking water, strange noises, or visible damage around the shower?
My Words: No leak. No noise. Just temperature.
English Conversational Partner said: That’s very clear, thank you. That information really helps us diagnose the issue faster. I’ve now logged the request with those details.
My Words: Okay.
English Conversational Partner said: You should receive a confirmation email shortly with a reference number. Please keep that email in case you need to follow up later.
My Words: Email is fine.
English Conversational Partner said: Great. If the problem suddenly gets worse — for example, if the water stops completely or becomes dangerously hot — please contact us straight away, as that could be considered urgent.
My Words: Okay. I understand.
English Conversational Partner said: Before we finish, can I just quickly check — is there anything else in the bathroom or apartment that’s been bothering you, even if it seems small?
My Words: Um… the fan. It’s loud.
English Conversational Partner said: Thank you for mentioning that. Is the fan loud all the time, or only when it’s been on for a while?
My Words: Only when long time.
English Conversational Partner said: Alright, I’ll add that as an additional note. The technician can take a look at both issues during the same visit, which is usually more efficient.
My Words: Okay. That’s good.
English Conversational Partner said: Perfect. So just to recap before I let you go: we’ve logged a maintenance request for sudden temperature changes in the shower, mostly in the evenings, and a noisy bathroom fan after extended use. A technician will contact you within one to two business days to arrange an evening visit.
My Words: Yes. That’s correct.
English Conversational Partner said: Great. Everything is submitted now. Thanks for letting us know, and we’ll get that sorted for you as soon as possible.
My Words: Okay. Thank you.
English Conversational Partner said: You’re very welcome. Have a good rest of your day.
